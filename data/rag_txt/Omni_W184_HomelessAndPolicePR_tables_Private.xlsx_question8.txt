Q3. Summary Good job: To what extent do you think that, generally, the police in the UK do a good or bad job at each of the following?

The purpose of this question is to gauge the public's perception of how well the police in the UK perform various duties. The key findings show that the police are perceived to do a relatively good job at policing major events like football matches (70% say they do a good job) and investigating major crimes (61% say good job). However, they are seen as performing less well at policing peaceful protests (57% say good job), acting as a deterrent for criminal activity (40% say good job), having a visible presence in the local area (36% say good job), and investigating minor crimes (33% say good job).
